IvyHacks 2020

Context

Duration: Jul - Oct 2020

With millions of students stuck at home, 6 Ivy Leagues took this opportunity to co-host the ultimate virtual hackathon. In 4 days, IvyHacks welcomed 3000+ students around the world to unite and solve global problems using technology.

Problem

How do we foster collaboration and learning in an isolated (virtual) setting and tone down IvyLeague's elitist image?

Solution

Through the theme Unity and Growth, we hope to bring students together during an isolated period and help them build off of each other to grow higher like the ivy plant.

Ideation Process

Deliverables

After rounds of changes, I transformed our ideas into 3D designs. As I was the most skilled in 3D software, I modeled all of the designs while helping others develop sketches.

Impact

In the end, the hackathon was a success!

Reflection

It was truly an astounding experience working with talented students from other schools. My biggest takeaways:

  • Be ambitious with ideas, but scale down during execution. I overestimated the team's energy and time commitment, so we needed to simplify a lot of the designs in the end.
  • Think ahead and "assembly line" simple social media posts. I made most of the 3D assets reusable pngs so the marketing members can easily create new posts.
  • Big events are hard to manage. Even with a style guide, not all posts are perfectly coherent. Don't be too hard on yourself or the team - reinforce standards and aim to do better on the next iteration!

Hack@Brown 2020

Context

Duration: Sep 2019 - Jan 2020

Hack@Brown is the annual hackathon run by Brown University and Rhode Island School of Design students. Through a friendly visual identity, we want to combat the stereotype of people going to hackathons only for the prize and not the learning experience.

Problem

How can we create a culture of inclusivity and growth to encourage even those who never coded before to join Hack@Brown?

Solution

The hackathon theme “Garage” encourages anyone with an entrepreneurial spirit to participate. 3D visuals also immerses the participant in a fun and "hands-on" startup environment.

Deliverables

3 designers, including myself, were responsible for designing the registration pages and dashboard. Specifically, I was in charge of the registration’s UIUX and planned the motion graphics for each screen, myself animating the books falling.

Challenges

1. Diverging from the landing page's vertical direction

Because it was hard to implement a registration page that jumps vertically, we added animations when the user clicks "next" to make the registration flow horizontally like one story.

2. Inclusive design for underrepresented group

I researched multiple gender/race inclusive surveys, including the US Race and Ethnicity Census to ensure that our registration selection options are all-inclusive. By advocating for inclusivity, I persuaded developers to remove the initial checkboxes and create a multi-select dropdown form that was difficult to implement.

Reflection

In the end, the hackathon was a success! We had 400+ participants and 1500+ unique website users at peak . It was a truly humbling experience and I loved my team!

To improve our designs and brand awareness for next year, I will:

  • Storyboard the user journey to improve UX flow
  • Adapt 3D renderings onto products - tote bags, sweatshirts, etc.
  • Identify goals to motivate the entire design process
