Feed Me

Donating excess meal credits

Product Designer
Front-End Engineer
November 2019 - Ongoing
Brandon Lee, Erica Li (Back-end Engineers)
Product Design Python Flask/CSS/JS Passion Project

Overview

At Brown, both freshmen and sophomores are required to buy a meal plan, which has an excess of meal credits. We wanted to connect upperclassmen who can't afford to eat off-campus or have time to cook with underclassmen who can't finish their swipes. This project was started in HackPrinceton 2019 and we are currently implementing it at a school-wide level.

    Check out our 1.0 deployed web app
    Check out our Github Repository

Problem

How can we connect students on-meal plan to those who are off-meal plan, while incentivizing them to reach out to one another?

Solution

Feed Me allows students off-meal plan (Eater) to buy a meal swipe from a student on-meal plan (Feeder), for a default $5. This amount will then be automatically donated to a charity.

Ethical Concerns

To verify our solution, I brainstormed the app's potential impact.

Competitive Analysis

Comparing our product to similar initiatives in the market, we are different because we donate 100% of the proceeds and focused on building friendships between Feeders and Eaters.

User Research

During the first evening of the hackathon, I interviewed six students from Brown University over text about their experience with the meal plan. The two personas are based on their responses.

Key Insights

The key insights align with my initial hypothesis:

Feeders give away swipes to friends off-meal plan generously, while Eaters prefer campus dining options because they are cheaper than restaurants.

User Flow

Based on the personas and our product's differences from other apps, I created the user flow to logically represent the user journey.

Design Process

With a basic UI in mind, I drew some rapid wireframes of features used by both users. Then, I conducted user testing on my teammates and noted their comments as captions under each wireframe.

Branding

Moreover, my decision on visual style was greatly influenced by the collaborative aspect of this mobile web app. Warm colors evoke the joy of eating, so orange and yellow represent the two users respectively. The gradient symbolizes the collaboration between the two users when a meal is claimed.

Hi-Fi Prototypes

After a few more iterations, I settled on the final design and the main functionalities.

1. Sharing a meal

The feeder will write their location, time frame, and intro message. A meal will show up on the Eater's explore page.

2. Claiming a meal

The eater can scroll through the carousel and choose a Feeder. Then, they can directly message each other.

3. Swipe confirmation

The feeder will confirm that they swiped for the Eater. A congratulatory message will be shown.

4. Payment confirmation

The eater will confirm their payment and the money from the transaction will be donated to a local food bank.

Challenges

1. Developing the Front-End

After iterating through a few designs and doing user testing on my teammates, I prioritized functionality and a seamless UX over visual design. We used Flask to tie the front-end to the PostgreSQL and Python back-end.

    Check out our Github Repository!

2. Implementing Security Measures

We implemented the login and post/claim meals user flows. However, due to time constraints, we couldn't develop a financial transaction platform with the Stripe API and a messaging function with the Twilio API.

    Check out our deployed web app!

What's Next?

We are currently working on prototype 2.0, aiming to implement a functional messaging and financial platform while addressing the aforementioned ethical concerns.

For future UX improvements, I would look into:

  • Represent accomplishments through visual rewards, such as badges
  • Design onboarding pages to help users quickly navigate the web app
  • Perform more usability tests to further enhance user experience
  • Implement a public bulletin for people to share their thoughts and attitudes towards the meal plan. This can be used to potentially advise the school administration and stop enforced meal plan for sophomores.

Despite not having a fully implemented prototype, my teammates and I felt a great sense of pride and accomplishment to create a program that people could tangibly use in only three days.
